What Veterans Need to Know About Total Disability Individual Unemployability in 2025

June 12, 2025 By Vocemploy Staff 8 min read

Total Disability Individual Unemployability — commonly called TDIU — is one of the most important and least understood VA benefits available to veterans. It can provide 100% disability compensation even when a veteran's combined rating falls below the 100% threshold. Yet thousands of eligible veterans never apply, simply because they don't know it exists.

What Is TDIU?

TDIU is a VA benefit that allows veterans to receive compensation at the 100% rate if their service-connected disabilities prevent them from maintaining substantially gainful employment. The key word here is substantially — meaning regular, competitive employment that earns above the federal poverty threshold.

"A veteran doesn't need a 100% combined rating to receive 100% compensation. TDIU bridges that gap when disabilities make meaningful employment impossible."

Who Qualifies?

There are two main paths to TDIU eligibility:

  • Schedular TDIU: You have one service-connected disability rated at 60% or higher, OR two or more disabilities with a combined rating of 70% or more (with at least one rated at 40%).
  • Extraschedular TDIU: You don't meet the rating thresholds above, but your disabilities still prevent gainful employment. This requires more documentation but is absolutely available.
Important Note Marginal employment — working in a protected environment like a family business, or earning below the poverty threshold — does not disqualify you from TDIU. Always consult with a VA-accredited attorney before assuming you don't qualify.

How a Vocational Evaluation Strengthens Your Claim

A professional vocational evaluation is one of the most powerful pieces of evidence you can submit with a TDIU claim. It provides an objective, expert assessment of how your service-connected disabilities affect your ability to perform the demands of competitive employment.

At Vocemploy, our evaluations document:

  • Your residual functional capacity (physical and cognitive limitations)
  • Your occupational history and transferable skills
  • The labor market availability of jobs you could realistically perform
  • A professional opinion on your employability — or lack thereof
